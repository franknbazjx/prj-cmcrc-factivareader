AngloGold mops up.
By PETER GOSNELL.

29 December 1999

THE scrip's in the mail for any investor still holding Acacia Resources shares.

The reason is predator AngloGold moved on Christmas Eve to compulsorily acquire all remaining stock in the top 10 Australian gold miner.

AngloGold's $800 million-plus share-swap offer, which had been extended, closed last Friday night with the South African mining giant boasting acceptances for more than 93 per cent of Acacia.

Under Corporations Law, AngloGold was able to now proceed and acquire the outstanding Acacia shares.

"I am delighted that our offer has been so successful and that we will achieve our aim of holding 100 per cent of Acacia," said Bobby Godsell, AngloGold's chief executive.

"This acquisition puts a substantial number of AngloGold shares into the hands of Australian investors and creates a market for our shares in this important gold producing area."

Acacia issued a statement to the stock exchange on Friday requesting its shares be suspended and the name Acacia Resources by removed from the official list.

AngloGold is required to make every reasonable effort to contact the remaining Acacia shareholders with its 3.5-for-100 share offer, either through personal correspondence or press advertisements.

Acacia Resources was formed in 1994 from the gold and base metal assets of Shell Australia.

It rapidly grew to become a 500,000 ounces-a-year gold producer, heavily reliant on its only wholly-owned mine, Sunrise Dam, for profits.

In September this year, its neighbour at Sunrise Dam, Delta Gold made a $640 million 1-for-1, all scrip takeover bid for Acacia, with the intention of creating a $1 billion gold company.

Acacia's directors stalled the Delta bid in the courts while they negotiated with AngloGold for a deal they felt would be more suitable. - SECT-Finance.
